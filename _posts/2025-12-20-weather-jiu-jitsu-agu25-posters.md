## Overview

I am presenting two Weather Jiu-Jitsu posters at the AGU25 Annual Meeting with Prof. Upmanu Lall's group at Columbia University. The work leverages the AURORA foundation model and adaptive perturbation to steer high-impact weather systems. This effort is part of my undergraduate research internship with Prof. Upmanu Lall's team in the Columbia University Department of Earth and Environmental Engineering. The collaboration bridges my SNU coursework in environmental hydraulics with frontier AI approaches for weather control.


## Poster Titles

1. **Weather Jiu-Jitsu: Toward Controlling Atmospheric Rivers with Deep Learning Foundation Models and Adaptive Perturbation.**
2. **Weather Jiu-Jitsu: Steering Tropical Cyclones with Small Perturbations of the Jet Stream in Experiments with Deep Learning Foundation Models.**
